## What we're doing

A ground-up visual redesign of the slot machine screen (the full-screen experience at `/slots/[pack]/spin`). The goal is a genuinely stunning, premium, expensive-feeling machine — not a checklist retrofit. All existing money/fairness safety rules stay intact under the new skin.

**MOBILE-FIRST (locked 2026-07-04):** the entire experience is designed for a phone held in one hand, portrait — that is the reference canvas every beat is choreographed on. Tablet and desktop are generous adaptations of the phone design (more air, same staging), never the source. If a moment only works on a big screen, it's redesigned until it sings on a phone.

**Brand guardrails (from PRODUCT.md):** hype, premium, trustworthy. The card is the hero. NO cheap casino neon, no coin-shower spam, no web3 gloss. Excitement comes from motion timing and reveal pacing, not clutter. Reduced-motion users always get a calm, instant version.

## The 10 locked features (agreed scope — not up for debate, only how they look/feel)

1. **Entrance animation** — the machine appears with a smooth choreographed animation when the screen opens, never instantly.
2. **3D reels** — reels read as physical wheels: top and bottom rows curve away from the viewer, the center row feels closest.
3. **Add/remove reels (1–3)** — reels can be freely added AND removed inside the machine (not add-only). Each reel multiplies the total spin price; the price updates live in both directions.
4. **Pokémon selector styling** — the Pokémon tiles ON THE REELS change from square sprite tiles to white, rectangular, card-shaped tiles (like little trading cards). Every reel cell is a mini white card carrying its Pokémon; the one that lands center on the payline is the card that later transforms into the slab back. _(Clarified 2026-07-04: "outer selector" = the reel tiles, incl. the center landing row. Clarified again 2026-07-04: SAME pixel Pokémon sprites as today — only the tile SHAPE changes, and the tiles use the real card aspect ratio so the reveal transition is shape-continuous with the slab.)_
5. **Tier reveal animation** — on landing, the tier's color first fades in slowly (same tier colors as the admin/tier setup), then transitions into showing a card. _(Clarified 2026-07-04: "tier" = the admin-assigned card RARITY, not the price-glow bucket.)_
6. **Landed Pokémon → card back** — the landed Pokémon in the center slowly fades and becomes the back of a card. You see the card back first.
7. **Tap to flip** — tapping the card flips it from back to front, revealing the reward.
8. **Multiple pulls review** — with 2–3 reels, all cards flip face-up, then you swipe left/right to review each already-revealed card.
9. **Sell within 30 seconds** — ONE shared 30-second timer for the whole pull (not one per card), using the admin-configured instant sell pricing that already exists.
10. **Auto-vault after timeout** — when the shared timer expires, unsold cards move to the vault automatically. Vault sales pay a flat 90% rate.

## Creative direction

**THE VAULT ROOM** — chosen 2026-07-04.

The machine lives in a private viewing room: a jeweler's back office / auction house at night. Near-black room, one warm spotlight cone from above onto the machine. Reels are dark glass-and-metal columns; as they spin, light sweeps and glints across the glass like reflections on a display case. On landing, the room itself slowly floods with the tier's color — stage lighting changing scene — then the card is presented under the spotlight. Hushed, theatrical, expensive.

**Accent rule:** the cards keep the holofoil magic (subtle rainbow foil sheen on card backs/fronts); the room and machine stay in warm light + dark glass. Foil belongs to the cards, light belongs to the room — "the card is the hero" stays literal.

## The experience, beat by beat

**0 · Arrival (~1.4s).** The screen opens onto a dark room. One warm spotlight cone from above, faint dust motes. The machine is _presented_: glass reel columns descend and settle one by one, left to right, each with a soft weighty compress; a thin metal payline sweeps across; a glint travels the edges; the controls rise in last. Reels are strips of small white card-shaped tiles, each carrying a Pokémon sprite; the strip curves away at top and bottom like a physical wheel — the payline row sits closest to you.

**1 · Configure.** Beside the reels, a ghost outline of the next column glows with a soft "+" (The Empty Pedestal). Tap it: the column descends (same Presentation move) and the price Meter rolls up with a clunk, shimmer, and a "× 2 / × 3" stamp. The outermost reel carries a small "−" handle: tap it and the column lifts back into the darkness while the Meter ticks quietly down. The math (unit price × reels = total) is always visible. Pedestal and handles vanish during spin/reveal — configuration happens only between rounds.

**2 · Spin (~2.8s; 3 reels ≈ 3.6s).** Ratchet wind-up — the reels pull up half a cell, loading tension — then release into a downward blur: white card tiles streak, glass reflections sweep the columns, tiles compress and dim as they wrap over the wheel edges. Friction takes over: cells tick past the payline slower and slower. Reels stop left to right, 400ms apart, each with a damped clack. The final reel alone gets a short suspense crawl, then snaps in with a half-row overshoot and pendulum settle.

**3 · The pause (450ms).** Total stillness. Total silence. The auctioneer's raised hammer.

**4 · The Room Turns (~1.2s).** The room's lighting slowly cross-fades from warm white into the **rarity color** of the pull (highest rarity on multi-pulls) — glass edges first, then the light cone, then the corners of the screen. A low tone swells; top tiers land a deep bass impact. The spotlight narrows onto the winner as the machine dims into shadow.

**5 · Transformation (~1.4s).** The landed white card tile — same card shape, same pixel Pokémon — **lifts off the reel and grows in one continuous move into the slab back**. Because the tile and the slab share the same card aspect ratio, the morph reads as ONE object changing size — never a cut between two different things. Mid-growth the pixel Pokémon on its face gently fades out as the slab-back details fade in (charcoal interior, plastic sheen, silver frame, rainbow holofoil Pokenic monogram, "GRADED · VAULTED" at the foot); a few light motes trail the move as an accent, not the mechanism. It ends floating center stage, larger than life, swaying gently in the tier-colored light. Multi-pulls: each landed tile morphs up from its own reel with a left-to-right stagger. _(Any tap from beat 4 onward fast-forwards straight here — theater is skippable, the spin is not.)_

**6 · The flip.** Tap: the slab lifts toward you, whips around its vertical axis with a glare of light at mid-turn, and settles face-up. Name, value, and rarity ribbon stamp in beneath. Top tiers get a knowing 500ms hover _before_ the flip and a fanfare flourish + shimmer burst after.

**7 · Review (multi-pull).** Flipped cards sit on the Gallery Rail: active card center stage under the spotlight, neighbors peeking dimmer from the edges, angled slightly away. Swipe glides with momentum; the incoming card turns to face you. "1 of 3" below.

**8 · Sell or keep (30s, shared).** The Auction Clock — a slim strip of light under the rail — drains right-to-left with a clear seconds numeral, strictly linear. Warm white → amber at 10s → gentle pulse + tick in the last 5. Each card carries its own Sell button (instant admin-configured price); selling opens the existing confirm sheet, then a clean glass credit note as the balance rolls up on the Meter. At zero: unsold cards lift, glide back into the darkness, a padded vault-door THUNK seals them, and a quiet line confirms — "Stored in your vault — sell anytime at 90%." No alarms; the timeout is a safe outcome.

**9 · Again.** The room eases back to warm white, cards clear, controls return: "Spin again."

### Layout & chrome (mobile-first)

**The phone (portrait, one-handed) is the reference canvas.** Roughly: top plate → reels center → controls in the thumb zone.

- **Thumb-zone controls.** Everything you tap in the heat of a spin lives in the bottom third of the screen: the Spin button bottom-center (big, primary), Odds and mute flanking it, the cost line beneath. The Empty Pedestal "+" and the "−" handles sit at reel-bottom height — reachable without regripping the phone.
- **Three reels on a narrow screen.** Reel cells shrink as reels are added (as today) so 3 columns + the 3D curve always fit a ~390px-wide phone with comfortable gutters. The curve and glass effects are sized to read clearly at those cell sizes — no detail that only reads at desktop scale.
- **Card presentation fills the phone.** The slab back/front takes ~75% of the screen width, center stage in front of the dimmed machine (the old desktop side-rail is gone entirely). The Gallery Rail is a native touch carousel — finger-following, momentum, snap — with neighbors peeking exactly one thumb-swipe away.
- **Sheets, not popups.** Odds and the sell confirm open as bottom sheets sliding up from the thumb zone (mobile pattern), sized so the card stays partially visible behind them.
- **Safe areas & short phones.** The layout respects notch/home-indicator safe areas; on short viewports the room compresses (reel window shrinks first, theater beats stay intact — time is never cut, only pixels).
- **Haptics are a first-class channel** — the phone's vibration carries the mechanical feel (ticks, thumps, fanfare buzz) that desktop conveys with sound alone.
- **Performance on phone GPUs.** All lighting/glow effects are built from cheap tricks (gradients, transforms, opacity) — no heavy filters that stutter or cook a phone. The theater must hold 60fps on a mid-range device; if an effect can't, it gets a cheaper design, not a lower framerate.
- **Top plate:** Exit, credit balance, wins — a slim engraved metal plate; balance changes roll like the Meter.
- **Desktop adaptation:** same portrait-proportioned machine center stage with more air around it; the rail gains arrow keys / visible next-prev buttons; hover states appear (pedestal glow, sell button lift). Nothing desktop-only is load-bearing.
- Tap targets ≥ 44px everywhere.

### Edge cases & honesty rules

- **Not enough credit:** spin blocked with a clear message + top-up link (existing behavior, restyled).
- **Interrupted animation** (backgrounded tab, hiccup): the built-in safety nets force-complete the result — the UI jumps straight to the card backs (skip-theater path). A charged player is never stranded.
- **Account switched mid-spin:** the entire result is dropped (existing guard, unchanged).
- **Reduced motion:** every beat collapses to ≤250ms fades; no scroll-spin (instant land), instant tier color, no sway/motes. Content never hides behind an animation.
- **Screen readers:** result announced once on settle (existing pattern); the countdown numeral is the accessible timer source.
- **Honest percentages:** the "90%" in vault copy always renders the admin-configured vault rate (90% today), never a hardcoded number — same for instant sell percents.

## Animation design decisions

- **Overall visual concept** — ✅ **The Vault Room** (+ holofoil on cards only)
- **Entrance** — ✅ **The Presentation**: reel columns descend & settle one by one L→R like display cases being placed; thin metal payline sweeps across; a glint travels the edges; controls rise last (~1.4s total). The same descend-and-settle move is reused whenever a reel is added mid-session.
- **Reel spin / deceleration** — ✅ **Momentum & Mass + final crawl**: ratchet wind-up (reel pulls up half a cell) → downward blur with streaked sprites and sweeping glass reflections → long physical friction slowdown (cells tick past slower and slower) → winner overshoots half a row and pendulum-settles. Reels stop L→R; only the LAST reel does a short suspense crawl before its snap. 3D curve compresses/dims sprites at the wheel's top and bottom edges throughout. _(clarified 2026-07-05 → #22: cells stream TOP→BOTTOM during the spin; wind-up pulls UP half a cell then releases downward — this is the canonical direction now.)_
- **Price reveal (add/remove reels)** — ✅ **The Meter**: price digits on mechanical rollers. Add a reel → the landing column's impact pushes the meter: digits roll up with motion blur, settle with a soft clunk, one warm shimmer sweeps across, and a "× 2"/"× 3" multiplier stamps in beside the price. Remove a reel → digits roll down with a quiet precise tick, no shimmer. Math always legible: unit price × reels = total.
- **Tier reveal → card back** — ✅ **The Room Turns**: full beat of stillness after the last reel locks → room lighting slowly cross-fades from warm white into the tier color, spreading from the payline outward (glass edges first, then the light cone, then the screen corners) → spotlight narrows onto the winner, machine dims into shadow → the landed card tile (same shape as the slab) lifts off the reel and GROWS in one continuous, shape-synced move into the floating slab back — the pixel Pokémon fades out mid-growth as the slab details fade in; light motes trail the move as an accent. Larger than the cell, swaying gently in front of the machine, foil catching the tier-colored light. Room stays tier-colored until the player acts. Multi-reel: room takes the HIGHEST tier's color; each tile morphs from its own reel with a slight L→R stagger. _(superseded 2026-07-05 → see decision #19: machine fully fades out rather than dims, card presents as a centered overlay)_
- **Card back design** — ✅ **Realistic Slab Monogram**: the back reads as a REAL graded-card slab seen from behind — plastic case sheen and edges, deep charcoal interior, thin silver frame, centered holofoil Pokenic monogram that shifts rainbow as the card sways, "GRADED · VAULTED" label at the foot. Not a flat illustration — it should look like the physical object the buyer actually owns.
- **Tap-to-flip** — ✅ **The Whip**: tap → card lifts slightly toward you → fast flip on the vertical axis with a light glare sweeping the face at the turn → soft settle; name, value and tier ribbon stamp in beneath (~0.6s). Top-tier pulls get a brief knowing hover BEFORE the flip and one restrained shimmer after landing.
- **Multi-card swipe review** — ✅ **The Gallery Rail**: flipped cards sit on a horizontal rail; active card full presentation size center stage under the spotlight; neighbors peek from the screen edges (smaller, dimmer, angled slightly away). Swipe glides with momentum; the incoming card turns to face you as it takes center. "1 of 3" indicator below; each card's sell button rides beneath it. Single-card pulls skip the rail (card stays centered).
- **Shared 30s timer** — ✅ **The Auction Clock**: slim strip of light directly beneath the card rail (lit-shelf edge) draining right-to-left over 30s with a clear seconds numeral at its tip; warm white → amber in the final 10s → gentle pulse + soft tick in the last 5s. One timer for the whole pull. Honest, always legible — factual urgency, never panic.
- **Auto-vault at zero** — ✅ unsold cards lift off the rail and glide back into the darkness at the rear of the room; a soft mechanical vault-door THUNK seals them; quiet confirmation: "Stored in your vault — sell anytime at 90%." No alarms, no red flash — timeout is a safe outcome, not a punishment. Selling in the window keeps the existing confirm sheet; credited amounts roll into the balance via the Meter.
- **Add/remove reel control** — ✅ **The Empty Pedestal**: idle-only ghost outline of the next column with a soft "+" at its center beside the real reels; tap → the column descends & settles (Presentation move) and the Meter rolls up. Outermost reel carries a small "−" handle beneath it; tap → column lifts back up into the darkness, Meter ticks down. Add/remove are mirror images. Pedestal and "−" handles fade out entirely during spin/reveal — reel count only changes between rounds. _(superseded 2026-07-05 → see decision #18: (+)/(−) move into the control row flanking Spin, always visible, disabled outside idle/review; the Empty Pedestal and under-reel handle are removed)_
- **Timing & easing** — ✅ **Approved masterplan.** Principles: (1) heavy objects, precise stops — expo-out arrivals, ONE restrained overshoot on settles, nothing rubbery; (2) spend time only where the story is — snappy controls/money, unhurried reveal.
  - Entrance: darkness 150ms → columns descend 550ms each, staggered 120ms → payline sweep 350ms → controls rise 300ms (~1.4s total).
  - Add reel: 550ms descend + Meter roll 450ms. Remove: 400ms lift + 350ms tick down.
  - Spin: wind-up 180ms → blur ~2s → friction ticks ~900ms → final-reel crawl ~500ms → snap + half-row pendulum settle 260ms. Stops staggered 400ms L→R. 1 reel ≈ 2.8s; 3 reels ≈ 3.6s to last stop.
  - Reveal: stillness 450ms → tier flood 1.2s → spotlight narrows 600ms (overlap) → dissolve/condense to slab back 1.4s ≈ 3.5s of theater after the last stop.
  - Flip: lift 120ms → whip 380ms (glare mid-turn) → settle 150ms → info stamp 200ms. Top tier: +500ms pre-flip hover.
  - Auction Clock: strictly LINEAR drain; amber at 10s; 1Hz pulse last 5s.
  - Auto-vault: lift 250ms → glide into dark 600ms (staggered 90ms) → thunk → confirmation fade.
  - **Tap-to-skip:** any tap during post-landing choreography fast-forwards to the card back(s). The spin itself is never skippable (outcome must visibly resolve).
  - **Reduced motion:** every beat collapses to ≤250ms simple fades — instant landing, instant tier color, no sway, no motes.
- **Sound & haptics** — ✅ **Diegetic base + big-tier fanfare.** Every sound comes from something visible — no music loops, no cash-register: ratchet wind-up, airy whoosh at speed, friction ticks SYNCED to cells passing the payline, damped clack + glass ring per reel stop, true silence in the stillness beat, low swell on the room flood, soft chime on card-back condense, crisp card-snap flip, mechanical Meter ticks, Auction Clock silent until the last 5s, padded vault thunk, clean glass "credit" note on sell. **Celebration is scaled up for top tiers (Mythical/Legendary/Immortal): bigger fanfare** — deep bass impact as the room floods, a proper fanfare flourish + shimmer burst on the flip, celebratory triple-buzz haptic. Common/Uncommon/Rare stay quiet-premium; the loudness is EARNED by rarity. Haptics: light tick wind-up → nothing at blur → subtle crawl ticks → medium thump per stop → strong thump on top-tier flood → light tap on flip → double-tap on vault thunk. Mute toggle silences everything.

Fix: the digit cell keeps `overflow: visible` and gets its baseline from an invisible in-flow anchor glyph; the rolling 0–9 column moves to a SEPARATE absolutely-positioned clip element. Baseline now comes from real text and lines up with the letters/decimals. Applies to both meters (credit + bet).
33. **Spin is a touch longer and lands naturally** (2026-07-05, feedback round 4) — (a) LONGER: deceleration is stretched (FRICTION 720→850ms, CRAWL 500→600ms, SETTLE 260→380ms) plus a slightly longer fast phase (BLUR 1400→1500ms) — ~+0.6s per spin (1 reel ≈ 2.9s→3.5s, 3 reels ≈ 3.6s→4.3s). The extra time is spent in the DECELERATION, not the blur, so the winner's start position (bounded by the fixed strip, esp. the 3-reel last column) doesn't overflow. (b) NATURAL LANDING: the old settle started the overshoot with a nonzero velocity while friction had already decelerated to ~0 — a velocity discontinuity that read as "decelerate, sudden stop, then a kick". The settle now EASES IN from zero velocity (`p²·e^{-kp}` damped-overshoot shape: quick dip, slow recover, zero velocity at both ends), so friction→settle and crawl→settle are velocity-continuous — one smooth landing with a soft cushioned overshoot instead of a jerk. `blurPx` is derived from FRICTION_MS so the blur→friction handoff stays velocity-continuous for any retune. All encoded in the vitest direction/continuity/bounds suite.
